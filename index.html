CA Consumer Protection Guide
CPUC Official Summary
OFFICIAL ›
Language
⚠️ Watch Out For False Claims
Most solar providers are honest and fair. However, do not do business with a salesperson who makes these false claims:
False Claim: You can get free solar energy at no cost to you.
Truth: Solar energy is rarely free. An honest company will be upfront about all costs over time.
False Claim: You will never pay an electricity bill again after solar is installed.
Truth: After going solar, you will typically pay a small electricity bill monthly or a larger bill at the end of the 12-month cycle.
✅ Know Your Rights
You have the right to...
•Review the 24-page guide before signing.
•A copy of the solar contract and/or financing agreement in the language the salesperson spoke to you.
•A Solar Disclosure Document from your solar provider.
•A 3–10 day notice of cancellation period.
❓ Ask These Questions
What is your CSLB license or registration number?
What is the total cost of the solar energy system?
If considering a loan, lease, or PPA, also ask:
• Is there a down payment?
• How much will I pay per month? When will payments increase and by how much?
If I sell my home, what are my options and what do I need to do?
Source: California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) · sce.com/notices

⚠️
Permit Poacher Defense
Mandatory. Protect every customer.
›
What's Happening
Once permits are submitted, your customer's information becomes part of a public permit filing. Unethical companies monitor these filings and use your customer's name, address, and project details to contact them — pretending they're affiliated with Riv Solar, the program, or even claiming to be a "follow-up" from your appointment.
This is known as permit poaching. It is predatory, it is deceptive, and in many cases it violates consumer protection laws. These companies are accessing your customer's information without consent to interfere with a deal they had no part in building.
Deliver This Before You Leave
"There's one thing I want you to be aware of. Once your permits are filed, it becomes public record. There are companies that monitor permit filings specifically to target homeowners who just agreed to solar — they'll call, send mailers, knock your door, or even show up online with ads."
"Some of them will use your name, your address, and details about your project to make it sound like they're connected to me or to this program. They're not. They found your information from the permit filing."
"These companies weren't at your kitchen table. They didn't review your usage. They didn't build your design. They're simply trying to intercept a deal they had no role in — usually by leading with a lower price without telling you what they're cutting out."
"If anyone contacts you claiming to be affiliated with Riv, the program, or your advisor — call me directly first. I'll verify it immediately and protect you from making a decision based on incomplete or misleading information."
Why This Matters
• Customers who are warned cancel far less often
• It positions you as their protector, not just their closer
• It gives them a reason to call YOU before anyone else
• It builds the last layer of trust before you leave the home
The Riv Golden Rule
"The best closers don't sell rates. The best closers sell certainty."
A homeowner who fully trusts the company, the process, the equipment, the provider, and the closer — will rarely make their decision based on one or two cents per kWh.
